<p><p>The marine composite materials market comprises several key types, each suited for specific applications. Carbon Fiber Reinforced Plastics (CFRPs) are lightweight and strong, ideal for high-performance vessels. Glass Fiber Reinforced Plastics (GFRPs) offer good durability and corrosion resistance, making them popular for a variety of marine applications. Foam core materials provide buoyancy and insulation while enhancing structural integrity. Meanwhile, metal composites combine metals with polymers for improved strength and resistance, serving specialized marine requirements. Together, they enhance performance, durability, and efficiency in marine environments.</p></p>
