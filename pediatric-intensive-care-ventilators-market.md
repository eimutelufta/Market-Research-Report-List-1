<p><p>The Pediatric Intensive Care Ventilators market is categorized into two main types: invasive and non-invasive. Invasive ventilators require the insertion of a tube into the child's airway, providing precise control over ventilation for critically ill patients. Non-invasive ventilators use masks or nasal interfaces to assist breathing without invasive procedures, making them suitable for less severe cases. Both types are essential in pediatric intensive care settings, addressing different respiratory needs and improving patient outcomes.</p></p>
