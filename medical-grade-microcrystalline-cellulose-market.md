<p><p>The Medical Grade Microcrystalline Cellulose market comprises two primary types: wood pulp-based and refined cotton-based. Wood pulp-based microcrystalline cellulose is derived from plant fibers, offering high purity and excellent functionality for pharmaceutical applications, such as excipients in tablets. Refined cotton-based microcrystalline cellulose is sourced from cotton fibers and is known for its soft texture and superior binding properties, making it suitable for topical formulations. Both types serve critical roles in enhancing drug stability, absorption, and overall product performance in the healthcare sector.</p></p>
